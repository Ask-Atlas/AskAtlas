KEY DATES TIMELINE — STANFORD HIST 1B
Renaissance to the Present

This timeline is a reference companion to the HIST 1B syllabus. Entries are chronological. Dates given as c. are approximate. Posthumous publication is noted.


THE RENAISSANCE (c. 1340-1600)

1341  Petrarch crowned poet laureate on the Capitoline Hill, Rome
1345  Petrarch discovers Cicero's letters to Atticus in Verona
1353  Boccaccio completes the Decameron
1378  Ciompi revolt in Florence
1415  Leonardo Bruni begins Historiae Florentini Populi
1440  Lorenzo Valla proves the Donation of Constantine a forgery
1453  Fall of Constantinople; printing press developed by Gutenberg (c. 1450)
1486  Pico della Mirandola's Oration on the Dignity of Man
1492  Columbus reaches the Caribbean; expulsion of Jews from Spain
1513  Machiavelli drafts The Prince (published 1532)
1516  Erasmus publishes his Greek New Testament; More's Utopia
1517  Luther posts the Ninety-Five Theses
1532  Rabelais publishes Pantagruel
1580  Montaigne publishes the first two books of the Essais


SCIENTIFIC REVOLUTION AND EARLY MODERN (1600-1700)

1605  Cervantes publishes Don Quixote, Part I
1610  Galileo's Sidereus Nuncius
1637  Descartes publishes Discourse on the Method
1651  Hobbes publishes Leviathan
1667  Milton publishes Paradise Lost
1687  Newton's Principia Mathematica
1689  Locke's Two Treatises of Government


THE ENLIGHTENMENT (1700-1800)

1721  Montesquieu publishes Persian Letters
1748  Montesquieu publishes The Spirit of the Laws
1751  First volume of the Encyclopedie (Diderot and d'Alembert)
1755  Rousseau's Discourse on Inequality
1759  Voltaire publishes Candide
1762  Rousseau's Social Contract and Emile
1776  US Declaration of Independence; Adam Smith's Wealth of Nations
1784  Kant's "What Is Enlightenment?"
1789  Storming of the Bastille; Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen
1791  Olympe de Gouges' Declaration of the Rights of Woman
1792  Wollstonecraft's Vindication of the Rights of Woman


ROMANTICISM AND INDUSTRIAL AGE (1800-1900)

1807  Hegel's Phenomenology of Spirit
1818  Mary Shelley publishes Frankenstein
1831  Hegel's Lectures on the Philosophy of History (posth.)
1848  Marx and Engels publish the Communist Manifesto; revolutions across Europe
1851  Melville publishes Moby-Dick
1859  Darwin's On the Origin of Species; Mill's On Liberty
1867  Marx publishes Das Kapital, vol. I
1883  Nietzsche begins Thus Spoke Zarathustra
1899  Freud completes The Interpretation of Dreams (dated 1900)


EARLY TWENTIETH CENTURY (1900-1945)

1900  Freud's The Interpretation of Dreams
1913  Stravinsky's Rite of Spring premieres in Paris
1914  World War I begins
1916  Saussure's Cours de linguistique generale (posth.)
1917  Duchamp's Fountain; Russian Revolution
1922  Joyce's Ulysses; Eliot's The Waste Land
1925  Kafka's The Trial (posth.); Locke's The New Negro
1927  Heidegger's Being and Time
1929  Woolf's A Room of One's Own; Wall Street Crash
1939  World War II begins


POSTWAR AND CONTEMPORARY (1945-PRESENT)

1945  End of WWII; founding of the United Nations
1947  India and Pakistan independence
1949  Simone de Beauvoir's The Second Sex
1950  Cesaire's Discourse on Colonialism
1952  Fanon's Black Skin, White Masks
1961  Fanon's The Wretched of the Earth
1967  Derrida's Of Grammatology
1975  Foucault's Discipline and Punish
1978  Said's Orientalism
1979  Lyotard's The Postmodern Condition
1987  Morrison's Beloved
1988  Spivak's "Can the Subaltern Speak?"
1989  Fall of the Berlin Wall
1994  Bhabha's The Location of Culture


END OF TIMELINE
